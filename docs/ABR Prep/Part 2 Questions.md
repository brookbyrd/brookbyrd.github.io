## Q1: Time dilation 
![[Pasted image 20260616154441.png]]

- Rocket speed: $v = 200{,}000 \text{ km/hr} = 5.56 \times 10^4 \text{ m/s}$
	- 1 m/s   = 3.6 km/hr
	- 200,000 km/hr   $\times \frac{1\ \mathrm{m/s}}{3.6\ \mathrm{km/hr}}$ = 55,556 m/s ≈ 5.56 × 10^4 m/s
- Distance: $x = 40{,}000 \text{ km} = 4.0 \times 10^7 \text{ m}$
- Speed of light: $c = 3.0 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$
- 1. Solve for time.

$$
v = \frac{x}{t} \rightarrow t = \frac{x}{v}
= \frac{4.0 \times 10^7}{5.56 \times 10^4}
\approx 720 \text{ s}
$$

- 2. Apply Beta factor for time dilation. 

	- For $v \ll c$:

$$
\Delta t \approx \frac{1}{2}\frac{v^2}{c^2}t = \frac{1}{2}{\beta^2} t 

$$

$$
\Delta t =
\frac{1}{2}
\left(\frac{5.56\times10^4}{3.0\times10^8}\right)^2
(720)
$$

$$
\Delta t \approx 1.23 \times 10^{-5} \text{ s}
= 12.3 \ \mu\text{s}
$$

### Answer:
The moving rocket clock runs slower, so it is:

✅**12.3 μs behind the balloon clock.**

# Shielding
### Q1: Lead Shielding Thickness

![[Pasted image 20260617211923.png]]Given:

$$  
\frac{\mu}{\rho}=0.0439\ \text{cm}^2/\text{g}  
$$

$$  
\rho_{\text{Pb}} = 11.35\ \text{g/cm}^3  
$$

Convert to the linear attenuation coefficient:

$$  
\mu=\left(\frac{\mu}{\rho}\right)\rho  
=(0.0439)(11.35)  
=0.498\ \text{cm}^{-1}  
$$

Using narrow-beam attenuation:

$$  
\frac{I}{I_0}=e^{-\mu x}  
$$

For 10% transmission:

$$  
0.10=e^{-0.498x}  
$$

Taking the natural logarithm:

$$  
x=\frac{-\ln(0.10)}{0.498}  
$$

$$  
x=\frac{2.303}{0.498}  
=4.62\ \text{cm}  
$$
 Answer:

$$  
\boxed{x \approx 4.6\ \text{cm}}  
$$
