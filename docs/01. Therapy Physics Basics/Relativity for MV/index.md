## Relativity Primer
Define:

$$
\beta = \frac{v}{c}
$$

The exact time dilation equation is:

$$
t' = t\sqrt{1-\beta^2}
$$

The moving clock falls behind by:

$$
\Delta t = t - t'
$$

Substitute:

$$
\Delta t = t - t\sqrt{1-\beta^2}
$$

Factor out \(t\):

$$
\Delta t = t\left(1-\sqrt{1-\beta^2}\right)
$$

For \(\beta \ll 1\), use the binomial approximation:

$$
\sqrt{1-\beta^2}
\approx
1-\frac{\beta^2}{2}
$$

Therefore:

$$
\Delta t
\approx
t\left(1-\left(1-\frac{\beta^2}{2}\right)\right)
$$

$$
\boxed{
\Delta t \approx \frac{1}{2}\beta^2 t
}
$$

So the moving clock loses approximately:

$$
\frac{\Delta t}{t}   = \frac{1}{2}\beta^2
$$

of its elapsed time relative with the stationary clock.
